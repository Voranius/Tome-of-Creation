Act II · 847 words · last edited just now

The throne room had not been cleaned since the siege. Kael could smell it — ash and copper, the particular stench of a city that had forgotten how to grieve properly.

Lord Varek stood at the far window, his back to the room, watching the smoke still rising from the lower districts. He did not turn when Kael entered. He did not turn when the doors sealed behind him.

"You killed three of my men," Varek said.

"Your men were burning a granary." Kael set the commander's ring on the stone floor between them. The sound it made was very small. "There were children inside."

Varek turned then. His face was older than Kael remembered — older by years, not months. The war had done something to his eyes that Kael didn't have a word for yet.
