Kaeladrin's backstory
Claude Sonnet ▾
What would drive Kaeladrin to betray the Thornwall Order after decades of loyalty?
Given that Kaeladrin spent 40 years believing the Order protected the weak, the betrayal lands hardest if it comes from discovering the opposite — that the Order's peace was purchased by sacrificing entire villages during the Silence War, a truth buried in the Veilstone Archives.

The internal logic: loyalty this deep doesn't crack for small reasons. It shatters when the thing you served turns out to be the wound you thought you were healing.
I love that. What if he found this out from an old rival who survived the Silence War?
A survivor-rival is perfect — it means the revelation comes with a face attached, someone Kaeladrin dismissed or defeated years ago. The rival having outlasted everything Kaeladrin fought to preserve gives the information both credibility and sting.

Consider: the rival doesn't want revenge. They just want someone inside the Order to finally know the truth before they die. That restraint makes them more devastating than any antagonist.
