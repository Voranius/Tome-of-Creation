Background

Aria Voss is a former scholar from the Greyveil Academy, specializing in pre-Sundering arcane history. She grew up in the lowland city of Threstwick, the daughter of a merchant family with no magical lineage — an unusual background for someone who eventually developed a talent for attunement magic.

She left Greyveil under ambiguous circumstances three years before the events of the story. The Academy's official records say she "withdrew to pursue independent study." Her former colleagues say something went wrong during a research excavation near the Veldrath borderlands.

Personality

Aria is methodical to a fault — she builds elaborate frameworks for understanding things before she'll commit to acting on them. This serves her well in research and badly in crisis. She keeps careful notes on everything, including people, which unnerves those who notice.

She is not cold, exactly, but she is contained. Warmth comes out sideways — in dry humor, in unexpected gestures of loyalty, in the fact that she has memorized the full names of everyone she's ever met.

The Letter

At the start of the story, Aria receives a sealed letter bearing Caldrath's sigil — a symbol she thought was purely historical. The letter contains three sentences in a handwriting she doesn't recognize, but somehow knows.
